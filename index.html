Fuzzy Dark Matter (FDM)

  • Extremely light scalar particle (m ∼ 10−20–10−22 eV)
  • Non-thermally produced (not ultra-hot)
  • Clumps to form Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC)
  • Quantum effects counteract gravity at small scales
  • Tiny mass → large de Broglie wavelength (~1/m) → macroscopic quantum effects at kpc scales

Also known as: F(C)DM, BECDM, ULDM, ELBDM, ultra-light axion DM (ULA, ALP)

Governing Equations

A. Wave Formalism (Schrödinger–Poisson)

iℏ ∂tψ = −(ℏ²/2m)∇²ψ + mVψ
∇²V = 4πGm(|ψ|² − |ψ₀|²)

B. Madelung Formalism (Fluid Representation)

∂tρ + ∇·(ρv) = 0
∂tv + (v·∇)v = −(1/m)∇(V − Q)
Q = −(ℏ²/2m)(∇²√ρ/√ρ) — "Quantum Pressure"

Q ill-defined at ρ=0!

Transformation

ψ = √(ρ/m) · eiS

ρ = m|ψ|²    v = (ℏ/m)∇S

Numerical Method: 2nd Order Unitary Spectral Method

  1. Calculate potential: V = IFFT[−k⁻²·FFT(4πGm(|ψ|²−|ψ₀|²))]
  2. Half-step Kick: ψ ← exp[−i(m/ℏ)(Δt/2)V]·ψ
  3. Full-step Drift (Fourier space): ψ ← IFFT[exp(−iΔt(ℏ/m)k²/2)·FFT(ψ)]
  4. Update potential
  5. Another half-step Kick: ψ ← exp[−i(m/ℏ)(Δt/2)V]·ψ
Kick: ψ ← e−i(m/ℏ)(Δt/2)Vψ

Drift: ψ ← IFFT[e−iΔt(ℏ/m)k²/2·FFT(ψ)]

V: = IFFT[−k⁻²·FFT(4πGm(|ψ|²−|ψ₀|²))]
